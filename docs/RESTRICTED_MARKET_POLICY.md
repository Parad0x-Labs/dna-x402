# Restricted Market Policy

The default marketplace is for paid APIs, agents, data, automation, and workflow tools. It is not a public gambling venue.

## Default Denylist

The market policy blocks obvious terms for:

- betting and sports betting;
- wagers, odds, bookmakers, sportsbooks;
- casinos, poker, roulette, slots, lotteries;
- prediction markets or event contracts offered as wagering;
- binary options;
- malware, credentials, exploits, botnets, DDoS, proxy abuse, and illegal access tooling.

## Why This Exists

Payment infrastructure can be neutral at the protocol level, but public marketplace distribution is not neutral. A public listing system that knowingly routes regulated gambling or illegal services creates operator risk.

## Future Restricted Mode

A future restricted mode must be a separate compliance product, not a hidden flag in the public marketplace. Minimum requirements:

- legal memo for target jurisdictions;
- licensed operator or verified exempt status;
- seller KYB/KYC;
- user age checks where required;
- geofencing;
- responsible-gaming controls;
- AML monitoring and suspicious activity workflow;
- dispute/refund policy;
- explicit risk acceptance by the maintainer.

Until those controls exist, restricted wagering/gambling listings remain blocked.
